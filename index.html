What this is

Not a journal. Not therapy.
Something in between.

Soul.o is a card deck that helps you locate what you're feeling, understand where it came from, and find a path through it — without writing a word. Three color-coded decks guide you down the right path based on what triggered you. No wrong answers. No pressure. Just you, being honest with yourself.

Opening the box

What happens when you sit down

1

Read the grounding card first. Always.

Phone face down. No distractions. The grounding card walks you through a brief breath and intention-setting. This game works best when you actually slow down before starting.

2

Choose your trigger deck by color

What set this feeling off? Pick the deck that matches.

Blue — It's in my head Warm — I did or didn't do something Violet — Someone impacted me
3

The deck walks you through a path — not a pile

Each deck is numbered. Card 1 is always acceptance. You move through: what happened → what you felt → what to do right now → what it might really be about. The path changes based on which deck you're in.

4

Find your Big Emotion. Place a sticker.

The emotion cards show the root feeling large, and all the secondary emotions that often show up instead — smaller, around it. Place a sticker dot on the one that actually showed up for you. Over time, you'll see patterns.

5

In the Moment card — then Dig Deeper (when ready)

Always get a tool for right now first. The Dig Deeper cards are optional — for when you're ready to understand what the emotion is protecting, masking, or trying to tell you.

It's in my head

Blue Deck · Deck 1

For when the trigger is internal — the thought spiral that won't stop, the comparison you keep making, the worst-case scenario you've run 40 times. The path here is about slowing the mind and separating thought from fact.

Card 1 always starts: "Accept that this thought is happening. You don't have to believe it — just acknowledge it's there."

Anxiety Overwhelm Dread Shame Paranoia Obsession

I did or didn't do something

Warm Deck · Deck 2

For when you're carrying the weight of your own choices — procrastination, something you said, something you avoided, a promise to yourself you broke. The path here is about accountability without self-destruction.

Card 1 always starts: "Accept what happened. It is done. You are not your worst moment."

Guilt Shame Regret Embarrassment Self-anger Numbness

Someone impacted me

Violet Deck · Deck 3

For when the trigger came from outside — a relationship ended, words hurt you, someone left, you were excluded. The path here moves through acceptance, then offers non-confrontational options: a letter, a release, or a boundary — never drama.

Card 1 always starts: "Accept that this happened and that it affected you. You are allowed to feel this. You don't owe anyone your silence — or a confrontation."

Hurt Betrayal Loneliness Resentment Grief Rejection
